About

I am a recently graduated PhD researcher working at the intersection of constitutional law, urban governance, and human rights. My doctoral thesis — The Constitutional Role of Municipalities in the Governance of Social Rights — compared how Turin, Barcelona, and Athens translate the rights to education, health, and housing into administrative practice, under the supervision of Prof. Giuseppe Martinico.

Before academia, I worked as a Policy Officer Assistant at the Delegation of the Canary Islands to the EU in Brussels, an experience that still shapes how I write: for readers who need to act on an argument, not just admire it. I currently contribute to I·CON (International Journal of Constitutional Law) as a Contributing Editor and coordinate social media for the Association for Borderlands Studies.

I work fluidly across Spanish, Italian, and English, with reading knowledge of French and German — and I am currently directing that range toward European policy and international‑organisation roles in migration and human rights.

Policy Briefs

A three-part series translating my doctoral research into operational guidance for city networks and municipal governments — distilling 300 pages of comparative constitutional analysis into what a policy officer actually needs on a Tuesday morning.

01 of three
Education

Local Government and the Right to Education

Why municipal competences, not goodwill, determine whether inclusive schooling becomes real.

"Where municipalities hold statutory planning and budgetary authority, as in Barcelona, inclusive education becomes an administrative routine; where they are confined to auxiliary functions, as in Athens, even the most resourceful local initiatives remain fragmented pilots."

Turin · Barcelona · Athens June 2026

Health

Local Government and the Right to Health

Municipalities act on the determinants of health everywhere; only some can make that action durable.

"All three cities act decisively on the social determinants of health — but only where decentralisation grants recognised powers and stable funding does that action consolidate into durable programmes rather than remaining donor-dependent."

Turin · Barcelona · Athens June 2026

Housing

Local Government and the Right to Housing

Why supply alone will not solve affordability — and why national policy can quietly undermine local effort.

"Without the legal powers to produce, acquire, or reserve stock, and without national governments restraining market-distorting policies such as Golden Visa schemes, municipal ambition runs up against constitutional design it cannot control alone."
